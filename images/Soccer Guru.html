The Ball Is Round?

Exploring English Premier League Match Data
to Understand How Teams Are Able to Perform Better Than Others

As fans of the beautiful game of soccer, our primary goal of this project is to understand how teams and players are able to perform better than others. We aim to create visualizations that would allow people to easily understand the metrics and performance indicators in an interactive manner. With our data visualization, we aim to provide a better insights in soccer like why is a team trending throughout seasons, how is it performing relative to other teams, etc.

The English Premier League is one of the world’s most popular professional soccer leagues, broadcasted in 212 territories to a potential TV audience of 4.7 billion people. It involves 20 teams from England, each playing a total of 38 matches every season, hence totaling 380 matches per season. The data for the project is a data set containing statistics for every match played in the English Premier League since 2008, making it a really rich source of information. The data set is available publicly here.




Fun Facts of the English Premier League

World-Class Players

The main attraction of the English Premier League is the variety of world-class players and the resulting different soccer styles among different teams. When there’s a FIFA break, many players from the English Premier League need to play for their respective country teams in international games.

Uncertainty of Result

“If uncertainty makes for compelling competition, nothing can rival the English Premier League.” - New York Times.
As a result of many world-class players, many of the Premier League games feature unpredictable outcomes. It’s very difficult to predict where the clubs will finish in the standings in the League table.

4/20: Champions League

Among the twenty teams, the top four teams qualify for the UEFA Champions League, which is the most high-profile club soccer tournament in the world featuring the best teams from Europe competing against each other from the group stages in September all the way to the final next May.

Squad Formations

>>Feel free to play around by moving the player positions to come up with your own formations. :

A formation describes how the players in a soccer team are positioned on the field. Different formations may be used depending on the skill of your opponents but also if your team wants to play more attacking or defensive soccer. For example the 4-4-2, is a formation with 4 defenders, 4 midfielders and two forwards, and is the most widely used formation in soccer today. Another example is the 3-4-3 is an offensive formation with little defensive thinking and most focus is put on attacking. There is no optimal formation that your team can use for every match.
